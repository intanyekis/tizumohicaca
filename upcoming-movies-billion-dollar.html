When we consider 2024's upcoming movies, it's hard to forget that amid hard times in the not too distant past, cinemas were effectively ghost towns.

Although theaters re-opened in July 2020 (with pandemic-related SOPs), movies like Bad Boy For Life (which had a huge fan base), Christopher Nolan's Tenet, and the Harley Quinn-centric Birds Of Prey (delightfully led by Margot Robbie), managed to gather a scant $426.5 million, $365.3 million, and $205.3 million respectively at the box office.

Those figures might look huge, but in Hollywood terms, they're not. Particularly in contrast with 2019, when nine movies reached the billion-dollar mark.

In all, 2019's billion-dollar films were: Avengers: Endgame, The Lion King, Frozen II, Spider-Man: Far From Home, Captain Marvel, Joker, Star Wars: Episode IX - The Rise of Skywalker, Toy Story 4, and Aladdin.

These figures demonstrate that before the pandemic, Hollywood and the cinema were in full swing, and then-upcoming movies were expected to achieve similar numbers the following year.

But in 2020, at the peak of the pandemic, the highest-grossing movie was The Eight Hundred, which earned a middling $461 million at the box office.

By the time late 2021 rolled around, the cinemas' situation began to improve, but at a glacial pace — just one movie, Spider-Man: No Way Home, hauled in a massive $1.9 billion, and the movie was released in the very twilight of 2021 (on December 17th, to be exact).

As it stands today, 2022 is considered the year during which the film industry began making real gains after a rough couple of years — moviegoers the world over genuinely missed the cinema, and they began to flock to the big screen to bring about the resurrection of "going to the movies."

In 2022 alone, there were three movies that reached the billion-dollar mark: Avatar: The Way Of Water, Top Gun: Maverick, and Jurassic World Dominion.

Then, in 2023, film fans eagerly embraced the cultural phenomenon known as "Barbenheimer" — and both movies, Barbie and Oppenheimer, collectively grossed more than $2 billion. (Barbie grossed $1.44 billion, and Oppenheimer grossed $957.5 million.)

The Super Mario Bros movie, also on the billion-dollar list, earned $1.36 billion — but at the same time, 2023 carried with it a new set of challenges, when much of the industry went on a lengthy strike.

2024 could be the year that finally returns a real downpour of money for producers, and it perhaps will finally and fully rejuvenate Hollywood and the movie industry.

There are many gems among 2024's upcoming movies, and to say that fans are excited about these films may be an understatement.

Some of these films are attached to franchises which have been cult classics for decades, and producers decided to extend the story of these beloved movies.

Dune 2 was one of the most anticipated movies of 2024, released recently, and steadily on track to earn a massive fortune, much like Barbie and Oppenheimer.

Let's get into the most anticipated upcoming movies of 2024, and see which films have the potential to earn a billion (or more) at the box office.

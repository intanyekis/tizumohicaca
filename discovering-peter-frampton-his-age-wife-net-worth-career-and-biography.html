Peter Kenneth Frampton, an influential figure in the English rock music scene, is a composer, singer, producer, and guitarist with a notable background of collaborations with well-known bands such as Herd and Humble Pie. His live album, “Frampton Come Alive!”, served as a testament to his remarkable talent and propelled him to international success.

Peter Frampton Profile Summary

Discovering Peter Frampton

Peter Kenneth Frampton, a distinguished guitar virtuoso, was born to Owen and Peggy Frampton in Beckenham, Kent, United Kingdom. His father, Owen Frampton, a teacher and head of the art department at Bromley Technical High School, cultivated Peter’s love for music from a young age.

Influences on Peter Frampton’s Early Music

Under his father’s guidance, Frampton was introduced to the world of Belgian gypsy jazz musician Django Reinhardt. His musical inspirations as a young boy included Cliff Richard and the Shadows, Buddy Holly, Eddie Cochran, the Ventures, Jimi Hendrix, and the Beatles.

Peter Frampton’s Musical Journey

At eight years old, Frampton began self-teaching himself to play the piano and guitar. He pursued classical music lessons, laying a solid foundation for his future career. At 14, he joined The Trubeats, and later The Preachers, both managed and produced by Bill Wyman of the Rolling Stones.

The Herd and Humble Pie

In 1966, Peter Frampton became part of The Herd, where he gained recognition as a young vocalist. His talents captured the media’s attention, leading to him being named “The Face of 1968” by Rave, a teenage publication. At 18, he teamed up with Steve Marriott of the Small Faces to establish the iconic rock band Humble Pie.

Rise to Stardom: Frampton Comes Alive!

In 1972, Frampton released his debut solo album, “Wind of Change,” featuring special guests Ringo Starr and Billy Preston. However, his live album, “Frampton Comes Alive!,” released in early 1976, launched him to worldwide fame. The album spent an astonishing 97 weeks on the Billboard 200 chart, 10 of which were at the top spot, and 55 in the top 40. “Frampton Comes Alive!” became the best-selling album of 1976, cementing Peter’s status as a rock icon.

Recognition & Achievements

With the overwhelming success of “Frampton Comes Alive!”, Peter Frampton earned widespread recognition and accolades. The album received a Grammy Awards nomination for Album of the Year at the 19th Annual Grammy Awards in 1976. His songs “Off the Hook” and “Black Hole Sun” also earned Grammy nominations for best rock instrumental performance in 2000 and 2006, respectively. Peter’s instrumental album “Fingerprints” won the Best Pop Instrumental Album Grammy in 2007.

Peter Frampton’s Net Worth

As a celebrated guitarist and performer, Peter’s primary source of income comes from his electrifying live performances. As of 2023, his estimated net worth stands at an impressive $20 million.

Peter Frampton and His Family Life

Aside from his musical accomplishments, Peter Frampton has experienced love and family life. He has been married three times and has three children from his various marriages. His first marriage was to Mary Lovett in 1972, which ended in divorce in 1976. Later, he married Barbara Gold in 1983, with whom he had two children. Lastly, he married Tina Alfers in 1996, and they have a daughter, actress Mia Frampton, and a stepdaughter, Tiffany Wiest. Despite the ups and downs, Frampton continues to be a loving father and a cherished musician.

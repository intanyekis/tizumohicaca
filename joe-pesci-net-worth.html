What Is Joe Pesci's Net Worth Now?

Joe Pesci has made his fortune from his acting career

Pesci is worth about $50 million in 2024. Most of that income came from his acting career.

Pesci started professionally acting in 1976. After getting a role in Raging Bull in 1980, doors opened for the actor like never before. Then he appeared in Easy Money (1983), Half Nelson (1985), and Lethal Weapon 2 (1989).

However, in 1990, he got one of his most famous characters, gangster Tommy DeVito in Goodfellas. Pesci had had a couple of run-ins with gangsters growing up around New York City. As such, scenes from Goodfellas are based on real life.

Joe Pesci Spends His Money On Real Estate

Joe Pesci made $5 million on the sale of his house and is looking to purchase one in California

The biggest thing Pesci spends his money on is real estate. In 1994, he paid $850,000 for an 8-bedroom mansion on the Jersey Shore. The 7200 square foot waterfront home features glass walls, a spiral staircase, an elevator, and a heated swimming pool. In 2019, however, he put it on the market for $6.5 million.

Pesci put his home of almost 30 years on the market because he "plans on settling in sunny California and booking tee times on golf courses." The home was built in 1990 in "the West Point Island neighborhood, protected from Atlantic storms on Barnegat Bay and only a short bike ride to the beach." He had a room full of his movie posters and a Lethal Weapon pinball machine.

The home eventually sold in 2022 for $5 million.

There is no information on whether Pesci has purchased his dream home in California as of 2024.

Joe Pesci Is An Avid Golfer

Since retiring from acting, Joe Pesci has become an avid golfer

Pesci is also a dedicated golfer, which is not a cheap hobby. It is pretty much all he does now when he's not making appearances and acting and making music once in a while.

"I played golf every day to keep my sanity," he told New York (via Turner Classic Movies ).

He's played with other celebrity golf lovers like Jack Nicholson and Dennis Hopper, as well as professional champion John Daly. Golf Digest says his number is 15.9.

8 Celebrities Who Were Accused Of Being Bad Customers

July 02, 2024
blog

Though it is easy to make assumptions about celebrities based on their music and the roles they play, it is impossible to make an accurate judgement about them until you actually meet them in person. If you haven’t been able to meet your favorite celebrities in person, social media can act as the next best thing. Social media has also allowed fans to hold celebrities accountable for their mistakes.

Related: Kylie Jenner Gets Slammed For Alleged ‘Rude’ Ride Forcing Zoo Patrons To Wait In Line

Some say don’t meet your heroes, and some workers will say don’t serve them. Social media has allowed workers to talk about their experiences meeting some of the biggest celebrities after serving them at restaurants and stores. While some celebrities have gotten rave reviews, others have been accused of being rude and cheap. Continue reading to find out which of your favorite celebrities has been called out for being a bad customer.

8 Hailey Bieber

TikToker @JuliaCarolAnn called out Hailey Bieber for being rude to her when she was a hostess at a restaurant in New York City. The TikToker said, "I've met her a handful of times, and every time she was not nice [...] I have to give her a 3.5 out of 10. Sorry." Hailey replied, "Glad u called me out so I can do better!! Hopefully we can meet again so I can apologize in person."

7 Kendall Jenner

Hailey Bieber's model best friend Kendall Jenner has also been accused of mistreating restaurant workers. TikToker @JuliaCarolAnn also accused the model of being "pretty cold toward staff." Kendall has also been accused of leaving without covering her entire bill and not tipping. Jenner and her legal team have denied these past accusations, but they haven't failed to give Jenner the reputation of being a notoriously bad customer.

Related: Brad Pitt Doesn't Mind Being Rude To Fans For This Reason

6 Beyoncé

Earlier on in Queen B's career, one waitress accused her of being rude - twice. The first time, she said that the star refused to make eye contact with her. The second time, the waitress said that the singer was "incredibly rude." A cashier also accused Beyoncé and her team of being rude when they put in a large order and did not tip. Other servers and fans have had more pleasant experiences with the singer.

5 Usher

Though Usher is most famous for his hit songs like "Yeah!" and "Love in This Club," he is also a notoriously bad customer. He has reportedly tried to get other people to pay for him. He has also thought that leaving his autograph was a sufficient substitute for an actual tip. A Target employee told BuzzFeed that when Usher went to a Target in Atlanta, he "was preemptively telling people that he wouldn't take pics even though no one wanted one."

4 Tiger Woods

Though Tiger Woods has made hundreds of millions of dollars playing golf, he is not as good of a tipper as one may expect. One time, he allegedly played $100K/hand at a blackjack table in Vegas, but he then did not tip the waitress more than $5. Tiger has tried to explain himself by saying that he just doesn't keep cash on him. His coach has also stepped in to say that Tiger is generous both with tipping and with giving to charity.

3 Scott Disick

Scott Disick was not only a questionable boyfriend to Kourtney Kardashian, but he was also a bad customer at one point. On Season 4 of Keeping Up With The Kardashians, Scott shoved a $100 bill in a waiter's mouth when he was in Vegas for Kim Kardashian's birthday. Kris Jenner stepped in to tell the waiter to stop serving Scott alcohol.

2 Joel Osteen

Though televangelist Joel Osteen makes his living by encouraging his followers to live more in God's image, a store worker told BuzzFeed that he was rude at a book signing. The worker wrote, "He was rude to every customer. He made outrageous demands of our store staff and forced us to call local police to 'protect' him."

1 Demi Lovato

Demi Lovato caused controversy when they called out frozen yogurt shop The Bigg Chill for offering "sugar-free" options. They argued that these options were potentially triggering for people who also struggled with eating disorders. The shop explained that the items were for customers with different dietary needs. Demi apologized after being criticized for attacking the small business.
